Folks that know me well know that I have Crohn's disease. I was diagnosed between the ages of six and seven, and have been living with this disease for the better part of the last 35 years. Until last year, my Crohn's has been relatively well-controlled over the last 10+ years. My provider was concerned that my treatment at the time, Stelara, wasn't at a theraputic level, and started considering options for ensuring my Crohn's would remain in a controlled state. Of course, insurance denied increasing my dose from once every eight weeks to once every six weeks, so we needed to consider alternatives to Stelara altogether.

I started Skyrizi last May. My understanding is that its mechanism of action is similar to that of Stelara, but with more power per dose. I was happy to try it since I'd still be able to do at home treatments after the initial loading dose infusions.

By mid-June, I'd developed abdominal pain. Earlier in the year, I tested positive for SIBO (small intestine bacterial overgrowth), so my provider and I were trying to figure out whether the pain was from SIBO or my Crohn's. To rule SIBO in/out, I did a course of antibiotics to treat it. It didn't end up helping, so we opted to start a course of steriods to treat my Crohn's instead.
